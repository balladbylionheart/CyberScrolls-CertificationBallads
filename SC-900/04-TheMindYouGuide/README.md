# 🎵 Scroll IV — The Mind You Guide  
**SC-900 | AI Shared Responsibility Model**

---

## 📖 Concept Overview

As organizations adopt AI-powered solutions, the **shared responsibility model** extends to include **AI workloads**.

AI introduces additional considerations because:

- AI systems are influenced by **user inputs**
- AI systems can generate **outputs that affect decisions**
- AI solutions can connect to **business data, plugins, and external sources**

This means responsibility is shared not only across cloud infrastructure, but also across how AI is built, configured, and used.

---

## 🤖 The Three Layers of AI

An AI-enabled application can be understood through **three layers**:

### 🧱 AI Platform
The **AI platform** includes:

- Underlying infrastructure  
- AI model hosting  
- Platform-level safety controls  
- Built-in safeguards such as filtering or access controls  

This is the foundation managed primarily by the provider.

---

### ⚙️ AI Application
The **AI application** includes:

- The app you build or deploy  
- Configuration choices  
- Data sources and connectors  
- Plugins and orchestration logic  

This is where your design decisions shape how the AI system behaves.

---

### 👤 AI Usage
The **AI usage** layer includes:

- How people use the AI system  
- The prompts and inputs they provide  
- Acceptable use policies  
- Human oversight and governance  

This is where organizational behavior directly affects the AI system’s outputs.

---

## ☁️ What the Provider Typically Handles

Depending on the service type, such as **SaaS** or **PaaS**, the provider typically handles:

- Securing physical infrastructure  
- Securing the AI model hosting environment  
- Managing compute infrastructure  
- Providing platform-level safety controls  
- Operating the underlying AI service securely  

These responsibilities apply to the lower layers of the AI environment.

---

## 🛡️ What You Typically Handle

Even when the provider secures the AI platform, you remain responsible for how AI is used in your organization.

### 📊 Protecting Your Data
You control:

- What sensitive data is shared with AI  
- What the AI system can access  
- Data governance and protection policies  

---

### 👤 Identity and Access Management
You determine:

- Who can use the AI application  
- What permissions they have  
- How access is controlled  

---

### ⚙️ Safe Configuration
You are responsible for:

- Restricting which users have access  
- Choosing which connectors and data sources are enabled  
- Logging outputs  
- Setting retention policies  

---

### 🚨 Mitigating AI-Specific Risks
A key AI-specific risk is **prompt injection**.

Prompt injection happens when malicious instructions are embedded in:

- User inputs  
- External data  
- Connected sources  

To reduce risk, organizations should use:

- Input validation  
- Access controls  
- Monitoring  
- Safe configuration practices  

---

### 📘 User Training and Acceptable Use
Because users influence AI behavior directly, you are also responsible for:

- Training users  
- Defining acceptable use policies  
- Establishing oversight for responsible use  

---

## 🧠 Key Takeaway

The AI shared responsibility model creates clarity.

As you move to higher-level services, the provider takes on more of the underlying infrastructure and service security.

However, you never delegate accountability for:

- Your data  
- Your identities and access controls  
- Your endpoint security  
- Your configuration choices  
- How the technology is used  

Understanding where your responsibility begins and ends is essential to securing AI in your organization.
